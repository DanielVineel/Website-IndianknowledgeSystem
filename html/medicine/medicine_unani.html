Unani Medicine

Unani medicine is a traditional system of medicine that originated in ancient Greece and was later developed in the Arab and Persian world before reaching India. It emphasizes balance in the body, mind, and environment for health.

Origin

  • Originated in Greece around 460–370 BCE with Hippocrates, and further developed by Arab physicians like Avicenna (Ibn Sina).
  • Introduced to India during the medieval period, especially under the Mughal Empire.
  • Integrated Greek, Arab, and Persian medical knowledge with local Indian practices.

Why Unani Developed

  • To provide a holistic system of medicine based on natural principles.
  • To maintain health by balancing the four humors: blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile.
  • To treat diseases using natural remedies, diet, and lifestyle adjustments rather than invasive methods.

Main Concepts

  • Four Humors (Akhlaat): Blood (Dam), Phlegm (Balgham), Yellow Bile (Safra), Black Bile (Sauda).
  • Temperament (Mizaj): Individual’s physical and mental constitution based on humors.
  • Prevention and Treatment: Emphasis on diet, exercise, herbal medicines, and natural therapies.
  • Holistic Approach: Treats body, mind, and environment together.

Therapies and Treatments

  • Herbal medicines, mineral compounds, and natural formulations.
  • Regimen therapy including diet (Ilaj bil Ghiza) and exercise.
  • Physical therapies like massage, cupping, and hydrotherapy.
  • Spiritual practices for mental well-being.

Cultural and Global Impact

  • Practiced widely in India, Pakistan, and parts of the Middle East.
  • Recognized under India’s AYUSH system of traditional medicine.
  • Contributes to preventive healthcare and complementary medicine worldwide.

Conclusion

Unani medicine is a holistic and historical system of healing that emphasizes the balance of bodily humors, natural remedies, and lifestyle, continuing to influence traditional healthcare practices globally.
